## 1. Passive cooling works — and it is measurable

**Source:** Systematic review of passive cooling strategies for arid urban
environments (Springer, *Discover Cities*, 2026; open preprint on arXiv).

- Passive cooling strategies group into **five families**: heat-gain prevention,
  natural ventilation, thermal inertia (thermal mass), evaporative cooling, and
  radiative cooling.
- Strong scientific consensus around three core principles: **solar control,
  natural ventilation, and thermal mass**.
- **Cool roofs** (reflective, high-emissivity coatings) lower roof surface
  temperatures by **20–40 °C** and can cut **annual building energy use by up to
  ~23%**.
- **Courtyards** create shaded microclimates and enable night-time radiative
  cooling; combined with high walls and water features they deliver
  **several degrees** of indoor temperature reduction.
- **Wind towers / wind catchers** channel prevailing wind and exhaust warm air by
  buoyancy, producing meaningful indoor temperature drops when inlet/outlet paths
  are well designed.

**How ETR uses it:** This is the scientific backbone of our *Passive Cooling
Recommender* and *Climate-Material Advisor*. We prioritise solar control, thermal
mass and ventilation before recommending any mechanical cooling.

## 2. ETR's own field evidence — Ghadames, Libya

**Source:** Dr. Ali Eltrapolsi, PhD thesis, University of Sheffield (2016),
*The Efficient Strategy of Passive Cooling Design in Desert Housing: A Case Study
in Ghadames, Libya* (free via White Rose eTheses).

- Studied vernacular desert housing using **questionnaire surveys + indoor
  monitoring + outdoor climate monitoring + building simulation** — a rigorous,
  evidence-based method we carry into client projects.
- Traditional Ghadames design (courtyards, wind catchers, **high thermal mass**,
  water-integrated microclimates, cross-ventilation) maintained indoor
  temperatures around **29–32 °C** while outdoor desert temperatures were far
  higher.
- Conclusion: vernacular passive techniques can be **adapted to modern buildings**
  to cut cooling loads in hot-arid zones.

**How ETR uses it:** This is our founder's primary specialism and our key
differentiator — *locally proven* passive design for the Libyan/desert climate,
not imported generic solutions.

## 3. Evaporative cooling — large gains, but humidity-dependent

**Source:** *Sustainable thermal comfort assessment of evaporative cooling systems
in hot and arid climates*, Scientific Reports (Nature, 2025/26).

- Evaporative cooling increased the number of **thermally comfortable hours** in
  peak summer by **41.3% (Baghdad), 54.5% (Basrah), and 30.6% (Mosul)** versus
  mechanical ventilation. *(Iraq case study — directional evidence for similar
  hot-arid Libyan inland conditions.)*
- Supply-air temperature was reduced from **43.5 °C to 26.3 °C (a 17.2 °C drop)**
  at 55% relative humidity — into the comfort zone.
- **Critical caveat:** effectiveness is **highly dependent on local humidity**.
  Evaporative cooling excels in dry inland climates and underperforms in humid
  coastal ones.

**How ETR uses it:** Evidence base for our location-aware recommendations.
Coastal Benghazi (humid) and inland desert sites get **different** cooling
strategies — this is exactly why our tools are location-driven.

## 4. Material selection must follow the local climate

**Sources:** Climate-responsive material-selection literature; hygrothermal
facade research; climate-responsive PV facades in hot climates (MDPI *Buildings*,
2025).

- Materials must be matched to local **humidity, temperature range, rainfall, salt
  exposure and solar radiation** to last and to stay energy-efficient.
- In high humidity, surface/temperature differentials regularly push past the
  **dew point**, causing condensation–evaporation cycles that drive
  **surface and deep masonry/facade decay**.
- Durable facades require a multi-layer approach: climate-responsive design,
  resilient material choice, adaptive facade technology, sound structure, and
  ongoing maintenance.

**How ETR uses it:** The scientific basis for the **Climate-Material Advisor** and
the **marble/finishing selector** — we screen materials for porosity, salt and
humidity resistance by *project location* before recommending finishes.

## 5. AI can predict comfort and outage-resilience

**Sources:** AI-driven prediction of building energy performance and thermal
resilience during power outages (MDPI *Buildings*, 2025); machine-learning
thermal-comfort prediction (Building Research & Information, 2025, trained on
the ASHRAE Global Thermal Comfort Database II, 61,000+ observations, 8 ML models).

- A **BIM + simulation + machine-learning** workflow can predict how a building
  performs — and how long it stays comfortable — **during power outages**.
- ML models reliably predict thermal comfort from building type, climate, season
  and ventilation strategy.

**How ETR uses it:** Foundation of our planned **"Power-Outage Resilience Score"** —
directly addressing Libya's #1 building problem — and our **Comfort & Energy
Preview** tool. Outputs are always presented as science-based *estimates subject
to expert review.*

## 6. AI estimating from drawings is real (with limits)

**Sources:** Independent 2026 testing of AI takeoff/estimating tools (Togal.AI,
Beam AI, Kreo, CountBricks).

- AI computer-vision tools read uploaded PDF/CAD plans, auto-detect walls, rooms
  and openings, and produce material quantities, **reducing takeoff time by up to
  ~90%**.
- Reported accuracy ranges roughly **85–98%** depending on drawing quality and
  building complexity.

**How ETR uses it:** Powers the **plan-upload → instant estimate** feature — but
delivered as a **3-tier "Estimate Ladder"** (instant range → smart quote →
human-verified quote) so accuracy and trust are protected.

## 7. Where the wider industry is heading (2025–2026)

**Sources:** Architecture-technology trend reports; generative-design + BIM
literature; 3D-printing and circular-economy research; construction-innovation
reports.

- **Adoption:** in a 2024/25 survey of 1,227 architecture professionals, **46%
  already use AI tools** and a further **24% plan to** adopt soon.
- **Generative & parametric design** lets teams evaluate many design options
  optimised for cost, climate and performance.
- **Digital twins** are mainstream for complex assets (airports, hospitals, smart
  cities), enabling scenario testing before physical change.
- **3D-printed construction** can build up to **~50% faster** and cut material
  waste by up to **~60%**; low-carbon options like **biochar concrete** are now
  proven in pilot projects.
- **Biophilic design** (courtyards, green roofs, natural light/ventilation) is
  proven to improve wellbeing, productivity and air quality — and overlaps ETR's
  courtyard research.

**How ETR uses it:** Roadmap features — generative concept options, a digital-twin
client portal, and a carbon/sustainability estimator — all sequenced after the
core MVP.

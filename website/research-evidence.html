Passive Cooling Saves Energy

up to 23%annual energy reduction (cool roofs)

Cool roofs cut surface temperatures by 20–40 °C; courtyards and wind catchers add several more degrees of indoor cooling. Validated across a systematic review of arid-climate buildings.

ETR use: powers our Passive Cooling Recommender.
Source: Systematic Review, Springer Discover Cities (2026) · open preprint

Proven in Libya — Ghadames

29–32 °Cindoor temp in desert heat (vernacular design)

Our founder's PhD field study measured how courtyards, thermal mass and cross-ventilation kept traditional homes comfortable while outdoor desert temperatures soared — and showed how to adapt this to modern buildings.

ETR use: the core of our locally-proven design approach.
Source: Eltrapolsi, PhD thesis, Univ. of Sheffield (2016) · free full text

Evaporative Cooling (Dry Climates)

17.2 °Csupply-air temp drop (43.5 → 26.3 °C)

In hot-dry conditions, evaporative cooling raised comfortable summer hours by 30–55% versus mechanical ventilation. Effectiveness depends heavily on humidity — which is why our advice is location-specific.

ETR use: inland vs. coastal sites get different strategies.
Source: Scientific Reports (Nature, 2025/26) · open access

Materials Must Match the Climate

Dew-pointdamage from humidity mismatch

In humid conditions, temperature differentials push past the dew point, triggering condensation cycles that decay facades and masonry. Materials must be screened for humidity, salt and solar exposure by location.

ETR use: drives our Climate-Material & marble selector.
Source: Hygrothermal facade & PV-facade research, MDPI (2025) · open access

Outage-Resilience, Predicted by AI

BIM + MLthermal resilience during power cuts

A 2025 workflow predicts how long a building stays comfortable when the power fails — Libya's number-one building problem. This is the science behind our planned Power-Outage Resilience Score.

ETR use: a tool no Libyan competitor currently offers.
